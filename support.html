FAQ

What should I add first?

Start with Wallets. Add your cash, bank account, e-wallet, or e-money card first. Then record income and expenses using the correct wallet so your balances stay easier to review. After that, add targets, loans, receivables, and create backups regularly

How do I add a Wallet?

A Wallet represents where your money is stored, such as cash, a bank account, an e-wallet, or an e-money card. After creating a Wallet, choose it when recording a transaction so the related balance is updated correctly

How do I record income?

Use Transactions and choose an income record. Add the amount, date, category, wallet, and an optional note. Examples include salary, business income, bonuses, or other money received

How do I record expenses?

Use Transactions and choose an expense record. Add the amount, date, category, wallet, and an optional note. Make sure the selected Wallet is correct so your balance remains easier to review

How do backup and restore work?

SaldoSehat backup files use the .saldosehat format. Store backup files in a safe place that you trust. Restore accepts valid SaldoSehat backup files only. Generic .json files are not supported for restore

What is an e-money card?

An e-money card is a prepaid wallet balance, such as a transport card, toll card, or prepaid electronic money card. It is not a credit card, paylater product, loan, or credit limit

What does notification privacy do?

Hide notification details changes only the notification content. When enabled, notifications still appear but use more private wording. When disabled, reminders can be more informative, but they should not show money amounts in the notification title or body

What is Ask?

Ask helps you read summaries based on data available in the app. It is based on available questions and templates, and it is not an official financial advisor

How do I report a bug or suggest a feature?

Contact support at saldosehat.support@maxqstudio.dev. Include the steps you took, what happened, what you expected, the page you were using, and device details when possible. Avoid sharing sensitive financial data unless it is necessary

Important note

Do not send export files, backup files, or screenshots containing sensitive personal finance data unless they are needed. If you send a screenshot, hide or blur private data first
